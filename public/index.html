About the Potomac River Crisis

The Potomac River, often called the "Nation's River," flows through Washington D.C. and provides drinking water to millions of residents in the region. However, it faces significant environmental challenges from agricultural runoff, industrial pollution, and urban development.

Our mission is to create a central hub where concerned citizens can find information, resources, and direct actions they can take to help restore and protect this vital waterway for current and future generations.

Key Issues Facing the Potomac

Agricultural & Urban Runoff

Excess nutrients like nitrogen and phosphorus from fertilizers cause harmful algal blooms, depleting oxygen levels and creating "dead zones" where aquatic life cannot survive.

Industrial & Wastewater Pollution

Chemical contaminants from industrial sites and inadequately treated sewage introduce harmful substances into the river, affecting water quality and aquatic life.

Biodiversity & Habitat Loss

Poor water quality has led to declining fish populations and reduced biodiversity, threatening the entire ecosystem's resilience and health.
